20 — Brand identity

Voice — do & avoid

Read the copy aloud. Does it sound like a committee wrote it? Rewrite. Does it start with the strongest idea? If not, restructure.

Do
  • Sound like a person wrote it.
  • Name specifics — countries, numbers, real examples.
  • State positions confidently, without over-explaining.
  • Discuss systemic AI impact.
  • Short sentences that land on their own.
Avoid
  • "We educate. We connect. We empower."
  • Corporate HR-speak and nonprofit boilerplate.
  • Hedging ("perhaps", "it might be worth considering").
  • Filler openers ("In today’s world…", "As we all know…").
  • AI product hype or commenting on launches.
Sentence case for headings. UPPERCASE for eyebrow labels only. Oxford comma on. British spelling. No emoji — ever.
21 — Brand identity

Positioning guardrails

Run every piece of content through these four before publishing.

Systemic, not reactive
Human impact on work, society, education. Not launches or news cycles.
Realist, not evangelist
No hype. No uncritical celebration. Emotional dimensions are our edge.
Human first
Technology is context. The human experience is the subject.
No political commentary
Systemic discussion, not partisan reaction.
